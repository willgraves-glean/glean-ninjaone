How the architecture works

The point is simple: models reason better when they’re fed the right internal context, under the right controls.

Step 1

NinjaOne systems

Docs, chat, tickets, CRM, engineering tools, knowledge bases, and internal people/context signals.

Step 2

Glean context layer

Unified index, permissions-aware retrieval, entity understanding, and reusable workflows.

Step 3

Best-fit models

Anthropic, ChatGPT, Gemini, or other leading models selected for the task and governed centrally.

Step 4

Trusted outcomes

Better answers, cleaner workflows, safer AI adoption, and less internal friction at scale.

Why teams buy Glean instead of building it all themselves

The real build-vs-buy question is usually not model access. It is whether you want to own the hard, ongoing enterprise plumbing around context and governance.

Dimension Build internally Glean
Context layer Needs custom connectors, indexing, ranking, permissions replication, and ongoing maintenance. Comes with a mature enterprise knowledge layer across connected systems.
Governance Requires bespoke controls for permissions, auditability, oversight, and rollout standards. Built around enterprise permissions, centralized controls, and safer adoption patterns.
Time to value Fast to prototype. Slow to harden, scale, and operationalize across the business. Faster path to production value across functions, not just in isolated pilots.
Engineering focus Internal teams spend cycles on non-differentiated retrieval and AI plumbing. Lets NinjaOne focus scarce engineering talent on customer-facing AI and product innovation.
Reusability Often turns into isolated team workflows or person-specific implementations. Enables shared, governed workflows that can scale across teams.
Model flexibility Varies by architecture and often drifts toward point-solution lock-in. Designed to stay flexible as model preferences and economics evolve.
